2. Malware and Keyloggers

Example: Malicious software installed on a user's device captures keystrokes or clipboard data, including private keys or wallet passwords.
Impact: Attackers can access user wallets and transfer funds without the user's knowledge.

3. Exchange or Wallet Hacks During Payouts

Example: Hacks on centralized exchanges or hot wallets occur during large payout operations, where attackers exploit vulnerabilities to siphon off funds.
Impact: Users' cash-out transactions are delayed or funds are stolen before withdrawal completes.

4. Insider Threats and Compromised Accounts

Example: Breaches of exchange or wallet provider accounts can lead to unauthorized cashouts. Attackers gain access via compromised credentials.
Impact: Theft of funds during withdrawal periods, often with little user control.

5. Price Manipulation and Front-Running During Payouts

Example: Malicious actors manipulate market prices or front-run large withdrawal transactions to profit from slippage or cause unfavorable exchange rates.
Impact: Users receive less cash than expected when converting crypto to fiat.

6. Fake or Unregulated Payout Platforms

Example: Scammers set up fake platforms promising quick cashouts, but once users send their crypto, they disappear with the funds.
Impact: Users lose their crypto assets entirely.
